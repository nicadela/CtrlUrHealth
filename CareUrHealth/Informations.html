1. What Is A First Aid Kit?

A collection of medical supplies used to give immediate care during injuries or emergencies.

2. Where Should I Keep My First Aid Kit?

Keep it in an easy-to-reach place at home, school, or in your bag.

3. How Often Should I Check It?

Check every 3–6 months to replace expired or used items.

4. Can I Use Any Medicine In The Kit Anytime?

No. Only use medicines as needed and follow proper dosage instructions.

5. What Should I Do In Serious Emergencies?

Call emergency services immediately before giving first aid.

Cuts and Scrapes

- Indoors: Wash your hands, clean the wound with water, and apply a bandage.

- Outdoors: Rinse with clean water if available. Cover the wound with a clean cloth or tissue until proper care is possible.

Bleeding

- Indoors: Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth for 5 to 10 minutes and elevate the injured area if possible.

- Outdoors: Use any clean material to apply pressure and raise the injured part.

Burns

- Indoors: Cool the burn under running water for 10 to 15 minutes. Do not apply ice directly.

- Outdoors: Cool the burn with water as soon as possible. Do not apply substances such as toothpaste or butter.

Sprains And Bruises

- Indoors: Rest the affected area, apply a cold compress for 15 minutes, and use an elastic bandage if needed.

- Outdoors: Avoid putting weight on the injured area. Use a cold compress if available and seek assistance.
